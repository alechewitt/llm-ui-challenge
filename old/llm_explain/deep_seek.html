Understanding Large Language Models

A simple guide for non-technical users

What Are Large Language Models?

Large Language Models (LLMs) are advanced AI systems that can understand and generate human-like text. You've probably interacted with them through tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

Think of LLMs as Super-Smart Autocomplete

Just like your phone's keyboard suggests the next word as you type, LLMs predict what text should come next. But instead of just suggesting one word, they can write entire paragraphs, answer questions, and even create stories!

LLMs don't "understand" text like humans do. Instead, they're incredibly good at predicting what words are likely to come next based on the patterns they've learned from their training data.

The Library Analogy

Imagine an LLM as a librarian who has read every book in an enormous library. When you ask a question, they don't recall specific books but instead draw upon all the patterns and information they've absorbed to provide a helpful answer.

What LLMs Can Do:

  • Answer questions based on their training data
  • Write stories, articles, or emails
  • Translate between languages
  • Summarize long documents
  • Help with coding and technical tasks

Important Limitations:

  • They can sometimes make up information (called "hallucination")
  • They don't truly understand what they're saying
  • They can reflect biases present in their training data
  • They don't have personal experiences or emotions
