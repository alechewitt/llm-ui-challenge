Step 1 What does a large language model actually do?

Think of it as “next-word prediction”

An LLM doesn’t “think” like a person. It’s very good at one narrow job: predicting what word is likely to come next, based on everything it has already seen.

Input = your message (prompt)
Output = model’s best guess of next words
Try it: guess the next word Interactive

What you just did Concept
You looked at the context (“The cat sat on the…”) and guessed which word fits best. An LLM does something very similar — only it has practiced on billions of examples and can do this for whole paragraphs at a time.
Step 2 How does it get good at guessing?

It learns patterns from lots of text

During training, the model sees huge amounts of text and practices predicting the next word. Over time it picks up patterns in language: grammar, facts, styles… even jokes.

From text to tokens
LLMs don’t see whole sentences the way we do. They break text into small pieces called tokens (a few characters or a short word), then guess one token at a time.

Step 3 How can you get better answers?

Good prompts = clearer instructions

3 quick tips Cheat sheet
  • Show, don’t hint: give a concrete example of the style you like.
  • Set constraints: ask for “3 bullet points” or “under 200 words”.
  • Iterate: if the first answer isn’t right, say what to change.
Big picture
A large language model is a pattern-spotter for text. It:
  • Reads your input (prompt)
  • Predicts likely next words, one by one
  • Uses patterns it learned from large amounts of text
You don’t need to know the math inside — just how to talk to it clearly.
