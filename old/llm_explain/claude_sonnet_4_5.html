LLM stands for Large Language Model. Think of it as a very sophisticated autocomplete system - like the one on your phone, but incredibly more powerful!

LLMs learn by reading billions of text examples from the internet, books, and articles. They learn patterns about how words typically follow each other, what makes sense in different contexts, and how to structure responses.

But here's the key: LLMs don't "understand" like humans do. They're incredibly good at recognizing patterns and predicting what text should come next, which makes them seem intelligent!

Predicting the Next Word

At its core, an LLM predicts what word should come next. It looks at all the words before and calculates probabilities for what might come next.

Try It Yourself!

Complete the sentence: "The cat sat on the..."

What word would you predict comes next?

mat (80%) roof (10%) elephant (0.1%) moon (0.5%)

The LLM does this same process, but much more sophisticated! It considers:

  • The entire context of the conversation
  • Patterns learned from billions of examples
  • Grammar and syntax rules
  • The meaning and relationships between concepts
